Building a product is easy. Getting users is hard.

After launching **[FeedbackMind](https://therobbieboo.github.io/feedback-landing/)**, I spent $0 on marketing. Here's what actually moved the needle:

## 1. Dev.to Articles

Write about your journey. People love authentic stories.
- ✅ Free
- ✅ Builds audience
- ✅ SEO traffic

## 2. Reddit Communities (r/indiehackers, r/SaaS)

Engage genuinely. Don't just spam your link.
- ✅ Targeted audience
- ✅ Feedback loop
- ✅ Community goodwill

## 3. Build in Public

Share your progress openly:
- Wins
- Struggles
- Daily updates

**People buy from humans, not companies.**

## 4. Product Hunt

Submit on Product Hunt. It's free and can generate 1,000+ views.
- ✅ Viral potential
- ✅ Backlinks
- ✅ Social proof

## 5. Email Outreach (Personalized)

Don't send批量 emails. Write personal messages to 10-20 potential users.
- ✅ High conversion
- ✅ Relationships
- ✅ Feedback goldmine

## The Results

| Channel | Views | Signups |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Dev.to | ~100 | 0 |
| Landing Page | 50 | ? |
| Reddit | 0 | 0 |

**Still early. But the foundation is built.**
